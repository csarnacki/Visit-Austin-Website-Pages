The South Congress Avenue Bridge is well known for the show that takes place underneath it every evening from late March to early fall.
The bats, which live in large colonies leave their roosts and fly out amoungst the sky in downtown, heading east towards Lady Bird Lake. The bats
typically come out at sunset and can take anywhere from 30 minutes to one hour to fully empty the space under the bridge. It is truly an incredible
sight to see as Austin is home to the world's largest urban bat colony. Becauase of this, the city rarely suffers from pesky insects that normally would
plague other cities at Austin's latitude.

Because of its hot and long summers, Austin's citizens are always trying to beat the heat during the summer months. Luckily, the city has two perfect water
sources for its residents to enjoy. The Colorado River and Lady Bird Lake both run through downtown and offer a perfect cooling
opportunity. Lining the sides of the river are numerous boating companies that allow people to kayak or paddle down the river, or, if they prefer, to simply
enjoy their lunch or dinner as they slowly cruise past downtown. Lady Bird Lake, located slightly east of downtown is a great place for people who are looking
for a little more privacy if they want to take a quick dip in the cooling waters.

If you are looking to get out of the hustle and bustle of the downtown core, there are some perfect places for hiking that are just a short drive from the
city's hub. Barton Creek Wilderness, McKinney Falls State Park, and the Texas Hill Country located to the west
all offer incredible views of waterfalls, canyons, bluffs, natural pools and limestone cliffs. The natural pools in the area also present a perfect opportunity
to cool off on hot summer days.

In keeping up with the outdoors trend, Zilker Park is one of the most well-known places to vist in the city. The park is usually used
for outdoor concerts and events due to its size and proximity to downtown. The park has disc-golf, volleyball sand pits, walking trails, boat rentals,
its own theater and a miniature train that traverses around the park. In addition, Zilker Park also has Barton Springs Municipal Pool. The pool is 100%
spring water and is a perfect dip on hot days as its chilly water, even in peak summer months will leave those shivering and wanting to relax and dry
out on its shores.

The University of Texas has its main campus located in Austin, just north of downtown. The university is well known for being one of the
more accomplished schools in the state and in the country where they offer a wide range of subjects that produce many successful graduates. Visitors to the
university can go to the top of the UT tower and gaze out at the 360 degree panorama of the city and its surrounding neighborhoods. Or, they can partake in
Texas's main pasttime; football. The Texas Longhorns are a beloved team in a city that contains no professional sports teams. The Longhorns are the main
show and football games at Darrell K. Royal - Texas Memorial Stadium are a must for any sports fan.

If you are looking for a peek into Texas's history, look no further than the Bullock Texas State History Museum. The museum is one the
finest ones in the entire state and focuses on the history of both Texas and Austin. The museum contains an interpretaive exhibit that rotates every few weeks,
an IMAX theater, a cafe and videos that showcase the history of the state. Due to the size of the museum, it is highly recommended to get there as early as possible.

The Texas State Capitol is one of the most prominent buildings in the Austin skyline. Due to its bronze color and its striking resemblance
to the U.S. Capitol, the Texas Capitol is easy to recognize from many parts of the city. Tours are available for inside the building and the grounds which the
building is located on is free to public access. Although, not used as the residence of the Governor of Texas as that title belongs to the Governor's Mansion
located just south of the Captiol grounds, the Texas Capitol is far more architecturally pleasing to the eye.
