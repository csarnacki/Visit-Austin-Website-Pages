Spring

Spring in Austin is characterized by very pleasant temperatures ranging from the low 70s in March to the mid 80s in May. May is the wettest
month in Austin with precipitation totals averaging nearly 4.5 inches of rain in the month. Spring also does bring in the chance for some severe
weather to the area. Austin lies at the exterme southern end of Tornado Valley and due to this and the Texas Hill Country that borders the
western part of the city, tornadoes are very rare. More common though are thunderstorms which can cause significant damage to parts of the city.
Spring also brings in one of the biggest events for Austin each year: South By Southwest. The large outdoor music festival lasts from March 16-20
and is located in Zilker Park and attracts massive throngs of music lovers.

Summer

Austin is well known for its heat during the summer months. Temperatures can easily reach into the 90s during peak summer, including average
highs of 95 and 97 degrees in July and August. On average, Austin experiences 116 days of days at least 90 degrees or warmer and, of those 116,
18 of them include temperatures that exceed 100 degrees. One unique aspect of Ausin's weather during the warmer months is its relative humidity.
The flow of the humidty levels are dependant on either the warm, moist air that comes from the Gulf of Mexico or the drier and lower humidity levels
that care associated with West Texas and northern parts of Mexico. The biggest event that takes place during the summer months is Austin's pride month
which is in August and includes a large parade that marches through downtown.

Autumn

Similar to the spring time, autumn in Austin is very pleasant with the temperatures having cooled off significantly since the summer months.
Autumn can also present the possibility of increased severe weather thanks to the transitioning of the seasons, however, as mentioned above, Austin
tends to suffer from thunderstorms than it does tornadoes. The largest event that takes place in the city during this time of the year is the
Austin City Limits Music Festival which takes place in early and mid October. Although not as large or as well known as SXSW, the ACL Festival
draws in large crowds in itself.

Winter

Winters in Austin are pleasant and cool with temperatures topping out around 62 degrees in both December and January. Austin does not typically
get snow, although trace amounts are occaisonally recorded. Instead, the city usually suffers from an ice storm roughly every two years. This ice
can be crippling for its citizens to drive in as they are not used to snowy and icy conditions. Temperatures rarely ever dip below freezing, however,
overnight lows can easily drop into the upper 30s which allows for frost to accumulate throughout the area. The Zilker Holiday Tree is the major
attraction during the winter months and takes place at Zilker Park on November 29.
